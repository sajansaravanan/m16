Uranus - The Ice Giant

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is known for its blue color and unique tilt.

This planet is classified as an ice giant, with a composition primarily of water, ammonia, and methane ice.

Uranus has a faint ring system and 27 known moons, each with unique characteristics.

It rotates on its side, making its axial tilt the largest of any planet in the Solar System.

Understanding Uranus helps astronomers learn about the formation and evolution of planetary systems.
